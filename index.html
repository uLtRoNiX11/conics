Circle

Standard Forms & Properties
Standard x² + y² = r²
General x²+y²+2gx+2fy+c = 0
Centre, Radius C = (−g, −f), r = √(g²+f²−c)
Diameter form (x−x₁)(x−x₂)+(y−y₁)(y−y₂) = 0
Parametric x = h+r·cosθ, y = k+r·sinθ
Tangent & Normal
Tangent at (x₁,y₁) xx₁ + yy₁ = r²
Tangent (slope m) y = mx ± r√(1+m²)
Tangent (general) T = 0: xx₁+yy₁+g(x+x₁)+f(y+y₁)+c = 0
Normal at (x₁,y₁) passes through centre C = (−g,−f)
Length of tangent √(S₁) = √(x₁²+y₁²+2gx₁+2fy₁+c)
Chord, Power & Family of Circles
Chord of contact T = 0 (same as tangent formula)
Chord bisected at (h,k) T = S₁ i.e. hx+ky = h²+k²
Radical axis S₁ − S₂ = 0
Power of a point PA·PB = PT² = S₁
Common chord S₁ − S₂ = 0
Family of Circles
Through intersect. S₁ + λS₂ = 0
Through intersect. of S & L S + λL = 0
Angle between circles cos θ = (r₁²+r₂²−d²) / (2r₁r₂)
Orthogonal circles 2g₁g₂ + 2f₁f₂ = c₁+c₂
Pole of line lx+my=n w.r.t. x²+y²=r² → (lr²/n, mr²/n)

JEE High-Yield Tricks

Key Observations & Short Tricks
  • Focal chord of parabola y²=4ax: If ends are t₁,t₂ → t₁t₂ = −1. Harmonic mean of SP,SQ = semi-LR = 2a.
  • Parabola normal trick: If 3 normals from (h,k) are concurrent, the 3rd normal foot t satisfies t₁+t₂+t₃=0. Point (h,k) lies on parabola if h>2a.
  • Ellipse: max area of inscribed rect. = 2ab (at θ=45°).
  • Point of intersection of tangents at θ₁,θ₂ on ellipse: x = a·cos((θ₁+θ₂)/2)/cos((θ₁−θ₂)/2)
  • Hyperbola asymptotes: Tangent at infinity. The curve and asymptotes differ only by a constant: xy = c² ↔ (x−ae)(x+ae) = b².
  • Reflection property: Parabola reflects rays ∥ axis through focus; Ellipse reflects from one focus to other; Hyperbola reflects toward the other focus.
  • Circle — 3 points: General form gives 3 equations → unique circle. Use family method for circles through 2 given points.
  • T=S₁ trick: Chord whose midpoint is (h,k) → write T=S₁ directly for any conic.
Common JEE Problem Patterns
  • Locus problems: Use parametric coords, eliminate parameter. Check: circle, parabola, or line.
  • Tangent from external point: Use SS₁=T² for chord of contact; length of tangent = √S₁.
  • Normal through a given point: Substitute y = mx−2am−am³ and find m using the given point. Can get 1,2 or 3 normals.
  • Intersection of two conics: Homogenize one with the line of the chord to get pair of lines → use angle condition.
  • Director circle: Circle → r√2; Ellipse → √(a²+b²); Hyperbola → √(a²−b²). Tangents from director circle are ⊥.
  • Eccentric angle trick (Ellipse): Area of parallelogram formed by tangents at 4 conjugate points = 2ab/sin(2α).
  • Rectangular hyperbola xy=c²: Orthocentre of any triangle inscribed in xy=c² also lies on xy=c².
  • Pole–Polar duality: If P lies on polar of Q, then Q lies on polar of P (La Hire's theorem).
